Research Projects

Data-Driven Labeling Approaches for Solar Flare Prediction

Traditional solar flare prediction models rely on the GOES classification system, which uses maximum X-ray flux as a proxy for labeling. This approach becomes problematic during solar minimum when background X-ray fluctuations cause high false alarm rates. My research addresses this issue by introducing innovative labeling regimes that incorporate relative X-ray flux increases and cumulative measurements over prediction windows. This data-driven approach reduces false positives and enhances overall prediction performance for both active region-based and full-disk flare prediction models.

Technologies: Python, NumPy, Pandas, scikit-learn, GOES Satellite Data

Related Publication: An Innovative Solar Flare Metadata Collection for Space Weather Analytics (ICMLA 2023)

Uncertainty-Aware Solar Flare Forecasting with Conformal Prediction

Current solar flare prediction models often lack proper quantification of their reliability, leading to frequent false alarms—particularly when dealing with datasets skewed toward extreme events. To improve the trustworthiness of space weather forecasting, I applied conformal prediction, a machine learning framework that constructs prediction intervals ensuring valid coverage without assumptions about data distribution. This work implements full-disk solar flare prediction using images derived from magnetic field maps and adapts pre-trained deep learning models with confidence interval construction methods. The results demonstrate that conformalized quantile regression achieves superior coverage rates and more favorable interval lengths compared to alternative approaches.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, Deep Learning, Conformal Prediction, Quantile Regression

Related Publication: Enhancing solar flare prediction with integrated multi-wavelength imagery and conformal prediction (AGU24)
